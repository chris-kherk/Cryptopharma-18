What is Tamoxifen(Nolvadex) 20mg Tablets?

Overview and Function

A medication that is primarily used in the treatment of breast cancer. Tamoxifen is available in 20mg tablet form as a selective estrogen receptor modulator (SERM). It operates by binding to estrogen receptors on breast cancer cells, thereby blocking estrogen from stimulating the growth of these cells. Estrogen is a hormone that can promote the development of certain types of breast cancer, particularly those that are estrogen receptor-positive. By preventing estrogen from interacting with its receptors, Tamoxifen effectively slows down or halts the progression of the cancer.

Tamoxifen's mechanism of action involves competing with estrogen for binding to estrogen receptors on cancer cells. This binding prevents estrogen from attaching to these receptors and exerts its effects, promoting cell division and growth. As a result, Tamoxifen helps to reduce the size of tumors and lower the risk of cancer recurrence. It is often prescribed to patients who have undergone surgery or radiation therapy for breast cancer and to those at high risk of developing the disease.
